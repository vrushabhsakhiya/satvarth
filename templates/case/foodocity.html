Foodocity

Some people will tell you there are four while others may tell you there are eight.

In the food and culinary ecosystem, people often encounter different ways of categorizing food experiences. Some frameworks simplify food into four core categories, while others expand the same ideas into eight detailed segments.

This inconsistency creates confusion for consumers, restaurants, and food platforms. Foodocity resolves this by treating food classification as a layered system rather than a fixed definition.

Objective 1

The Four-Category Food Model The four-category model focuses on simplicity. It helps users quickly understand broad food preferences, cuisines, and dining styles without overload.

Objective 2

The Eight-Category Food Model The eight-category model adds precision by breaking each core food category into refined segments, enabling better recommendations and personalization.

Objective 3

Foodocity’s Adaptive Classification Foodocity does not force a single structure. It dynamically applies either the four-category or eight-category model based on user intent and depth of exploration.
Platform

Foodocity Culinary Intelligence System

Core Purpose

Smart food discovery and structured culinary insights

Primary Users

Food lovers, restaurants, chefs, and food-tech platforms

Conclusion

Four categories offer clarity. Eight categories deliver accuracy. Foodocity succeeds by using both — depending on taste, context, and depth.
