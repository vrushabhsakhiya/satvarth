2024 Top SEO Features for Marketing Designs

Written by

James Smith

In 2024, SEO is no longer just about keywords — it's tightly connected with design decisions. Marketing teams now expect UI/UX designers to understand how layout, structure, and content formats influence search performance.

A visually appealing website means nothing if Google can't read, index, or understand it. And in 2024, search algorithms prioritize clarity, structure, and interaction patterns more than ever.


1. Search-Optimized Layout Structures

Google's algorithms now evaluate how quickly users find information. Pages with clear spacing, readable sections, and consistent hierarchy perform better. Designers must use clean HTML structure, headings, and semantic sections.


2. Core Web Vitals Integration

Speed and stability now directly impact rankings. Lightweight design, compressed components, and reduced scripts improve LCP, CLS, and FID. Designers must collaborate with developers from the start.


3. SEO-Focused Component Design

Every component should be built with search discoverability in mind — accordions, tables, CTAs, and FAQ sections all require proper tags and structured content to rank.

  • Use proper heading hierarchy (H1 → H2 → H3)
  • Design for fast loading (no heavy animations)
  • Ensure text, not images, for important content
  • Create modular sections for easy indexing

"SEO and design are no longer separate tasks. In 2024, the winning teams are the ones who build for users AND search engines together."


4. Enhanced Mobile UX for Higher Rankings

Google crawls mobile-first. If your layout breaks, loads slowly, or hides key content on smaller screens, your ranking drops instantly. Designing for responsiveness is now directly tied to SEO success.
